Kinetics & Collision Theory Summary

Concept Graphs 1 & 2 (Thermodynamics) Graph 3 (Catalysis) Graph 4 (Temperature)
Activation Energy (Ea) A fixed energy barrier the molecules must overcome to react. A catalyst acts as a shortcut, physically lowering the height of the Ea barrier. Temperature does not change the barrier. The Ea line stays exactly the same.
Kinetic Energy (KE) Converted to PE to break bonds, then created as new bonds form. Molecules need less KE to successfully cross the lowered barrier. Heating the system gives the entire population a higher average KE, shifting the curve right.
Why is the reaction faster? N/A (Depends on temp/catalyst) Because the barrier is smaller, a higher % of normal molecules can easily cross it. Because the molecules are faster, a higher % of them now have enough energy to cross the normal barrier.
